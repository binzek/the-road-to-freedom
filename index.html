The Road to Freedom: India's Journey to Independence

India's freedom fight was a long struggle against British colonial rule, led by prominent freedom fighters who used various methods of resistance. The fight culminated in the Indian Independence Act of 1947, which granted India its freedom. Explore the timeline to learn more about this pivotal period in Indian history.

1857

Indian Rebellion

A widespread uprising against British rule in India that lasted from May 1857 to June 1858. It was the first significant attempt by Indians to throw off British rule.

1885

Formation of Indian National Congress

The Indian National Congress was formed in December 1885 by a group of educated Indians who wanted to create a platform for political engagement and representation for the Indian people.

1919

Jallianwala Bagh massacre

A public massacre of peaceful protestors by British Indian Army soldiers in Amritsar, Punjab, on April 13, 1919. The brutal attack left hundreds dead and thousands injured, and became a turning point in India's struggle for independence.

1920

Non-Cooperation Movement

A mass civil disobedience campaign launched by Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian National Congress in 1920 against the British colonial government in India. The movement called for Indians to boycott British goods, schools, and institutions, and eventually led to widespread protests and mass arrests.

1930

Salt Satyagraha

A campaign of nonviolent resistance against British salt tax in colonial India, led by Mahatma Gandhi. The Salt Satyagraha began with the famous Dandi March in March 1930, and resulted in the British authorities arresting tens of thousands of Indian activists and leaders.

1942

Quit India Movement

A mass civil disobedience movement launched by Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian National Congress in August 1942 demanding an end to British colonial rule in India. The movement led to widespread protests, strikes, and acts of sabotage, resulting in the arrest of over 100,000 people.

1947

Indian Independence Act

The Indian Independence Act of 1947 was an Act of the Parliament of the United Kingdom that partitioned British India into two independent dominions of India and Pakistan. The Act came into effect on August 15, 1947, marking the end of British colonial rule in India.
